Problem Identification

  • What are the effects of a natural setting on psychedelic experiences?
  • Does a natural setting impact the psychedelic experience?
  • How meaningful is a psychedelic experience in a nature-based setting?
  • How much have psychedelics impacted the consumer’s use of nature?
  • Does taking psychedelics in nature elicit awe?
Research Rationale

  • To explore the impact of set and setting
  • To discuss a topic that is generally considered socially taboo
  • To further the understanding of the researchers for consideration of their own careers
  • To explore and deepen the understanding of ego-dissolution
Collaboration Process

  • Collective recognition of the power of psychedelics
  • Deep understanding of connection to the natural world as a psychological resource

  • Personal experiences as a guide to finding research that either supported or disproved our belief that meaningful* psychedelic experiences were positively correlated with increased feelings of connection to nature

Literature Review

PSYCHOLOGICAL WELL-BEING & PERSONAL CONNECTEDNESS TO NATURE - Wolsko & Lindberg (2013)

  • Assessed relationship between the Connectedness to Nature Scale (CNS), psychological well-being, mindfulness, and outdoor recreation
  • CNS assess emotional connection to nature and “experiential sense of oneness with the natural world” (Mayer & Frantz, 2004, p. 504)
  • Higher CNS scores were associated with higher scores on both hedonic (what is pleasurable, i.e. positive emotions) and eudaimonic (what is meaningful, i.e. experiencing living a fulfilling life) aspects of well-being
  • Individuals who reported experiencing more connection with nature reported higher levels of fulfillment in significant life areas (i.e., interpersonal relationships, self-esteem, ect.) subjective vitality, and positive emotions
  • The study indicates that individuals who experience stronger feelings of connection with nature are likely to have greater psychological well-being
Literature Review

CRITICAL REVIEW: STPE, MEANINGFUL PSYCHEDELIC EXPERIENCES & PERSONAL CONNECTEDNESS TO NATURE - Newton & Moreton (2022)

  • Mystical experiences, ego-dissolution (ED), and self-transcendent positive emotions (STPE) during meaningful psychedelic experiences were positively correlated with increased feelings of connection to nature as measured by CNS and Inclusion of Nature in Self scale (INS) scores.
  • Findings suggest that feelings of gratitude, compassion, and awe (STPE) during psychedelic experiences, specifically, can have a bottom-down effect, and are not only positively correlated with connectedness to nature, but also affect an individual's disposition to experience STPE outside of the psychedelic experience in the future.
Literature Review

SYNERGISTIC EFFECTS OF PSYCHEDELICS & NATURE FOR MENTAL HEALTH - Gandy et al. (2020)

  • Psychedelics have the potential to increase nature relatedness or connectedness, which is associated with psychological health and well-being
  • Contact with nature and measures of nature connectedness are associated with many psychological benefits and are strong predictors of psychological well-being
  • Both psychedelics and contact with nature:
    • reduce rumination and activity in brain regions involved in depression
    • have the capacity to increase a sense of connectedness
    • increase the quality trait of openness
    • foster mindful capacities
    • reduce negative affect
    • elicit awe
  • Psychedelic use with the intent to connect with nature have been associated with greater well-being scores in comparison to other motivations behind use
All these are linked to enhanced psychological well-being. Separately, both psychedelics and nature contact can act as amplifiers for the therapeutic process, so together they have synergetic benefits. *Eudaimonic well-being is defined by authors as: subjective experiences linked to living a life of virtue in pursuit of human excellence, associated with experiences of self-actualisation, vitality and personal expressiveness. *ED is “‘a disruption of ego-boundaries, which results in a blurring of the distinction between self-representation and object-representation’ (Nour et al., 2016) and is strongly associated with nature relatedness.”

Results & Implications

  • 17.65% of respondents had no experience with psychedelics
  • 29.41% of respondents reported no impact on use of nature
  • 100.0% of respondents reported strong nature connections

Research Takeaways

  • Psychedelic experiences and nature contact have many overlapping effects and benefits that impact psychological well-being
  • Together they may act as amplifiers in the therapeutic process
  • The acute effects of psychedelic experiences may influence both the inclination to use psychedelics in a natural setting and also reinforce a relationship with nature
Both promote feelings of connectedness, interconnectedness, oneness, and overall well-being.
Clinical Applications

  • Incorporating nature into therapy as a resource and source of psychological well-being
    • Nature elements in office, view out into the natural environment, nature space in the vicinity of the place of clinical practice
  • Incorporating nature into set and setting, especially if the psychedelic experience must be conducted in a clinical setting due to safety concerns
    • Nature metaphors
    • Symbolic rituals within/in nature indicating preparation or integration
  • Incorporating and integrating clients’ psychedelic experiences and contact or relationship with nature to enrich the therapeutic process
    • Nature as a resource
    • Tapping into existing feelings of connectedness, interconnectedness, and oneness to foster greater psychological well-being
    • Developing complementary practices, such as, mindfulness practices, breathing, grounding techniques, and resourcing, to deepen and strengthen
