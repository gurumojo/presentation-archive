Clinical Social Justice: Implications in Practice

The American Counseling Association has endorsed competencies developed by Counselors for Social Justice.

Knowing that individuals' experiences and behaviors can only be understood accurately in context, we have the professional responsibility [to avoid ignoring] clients' social and cultural contexts when working with them. (Unknown, 2016)

The "Social Conscience of ACA" curates educational content, organizes networking conferences, and provides therapists with clear calls to action.

As seen at the page linked above, CSJ provides templates including items for prior consideration, talking point script examples, and clear messaging about the opptions suggested for responding to a call to action:

CALL, CONNECT, COLLABORATE, COMMUNICATE, and CONTRIBUTE.

Ultimately, harm-free relationships are best maintained by embracing an identity focused on social justice.

"Social justice is not about what we do, it's about who we are."
- Dr. Michael Hutchins, Co-Founder of Counselors for Social Justice
